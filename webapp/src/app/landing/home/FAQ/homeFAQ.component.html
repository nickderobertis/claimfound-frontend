Unclaimed Property Overview

What exactly is unclaimed property?

“Unclaimed property,” also known as “unclaimed money,” refers to misplaced money that has been turned over to a government agency (typically the state treasury) after a period of inactivity between the money and the person who it belongs to originally.
Examples of unclaimed property include: forgotten utility deposits, dorman bank accounts, back taxes, rebates, life insurance policies of deceased relatives, dividends, and more than 500 other examples.
Learn more here - Understanding Unclaimed Money

I'm an organized person, how did I lose my money?

Most people don't think they could have any unclaimed money because they tend to be organized, but that mean things still don't fall through the cracks. There wouldn't be $70+ billion in lost money if everyone was as organized as they thought.
Any time money or assets change hands it can be “lost in transit.” Whether your former employer mailed your last paycheck to the wrong address, you forgot about an old savings account, or you didn’t know about a deceased relative’s life insurance policy… there are literally hundreds of ways money can become unclaimed.

Is unclaimed proeprty a scam?

No, unclaimed property is legitimate and managed by federal and state government agencies. If you don’t believe us Google “Unclaimed Property Florida” and you’ll find a state sponsored website.
However, Unclaimed Property is an industry surrounded by misinformation and skepticism. There are many reported cases of individuals who fraudulently try to claim other peoples money each year. You may have even received a letter in the mail by someone trying to help you get your money back for a fee.
Learn more about how to identify scammers.

How much unclaimed money is out there?

Although the exact dollar amount is not published, based on ClaimFound’s research we believe there is more than $70 billion held by state agencies and more than $26 billion held by the federal government.
Every year the amount of new unclaimed property increases. In 2018 new unclaimed property across the U.S. totalled $7.1 billion and in 2019 it increased to over $8 billion.
We conservatively estimate there are at least 100 million Americans that have unclaimed money either owed directly to them or to a deceased family member for which you can claim their money.

Can I claim someone else's unclaimed money?

Yes, if the person is a deceased family member, or if you are listed on the will of the deceased individual, you can make a claim to retrieve their unclaimed money.
There are extra steps in claiming your deceased family members' money though, typically you will need to provide a modified will to the unclaimed property department that shows the unclaimed property is listed on it and who it should be dispersed to.
